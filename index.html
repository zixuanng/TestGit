Latent Disease State Trajectories

Reveal hidden disease patterns before symptoms appear.

Latent Disease State Trajectories (LDST) is a framework for modeling the unseen progression of chronic conditions using longitudinal data, machine learning, and clinical expertise. It helps researchers and care teams understand where a patient is on their journey and what comes next.

Explore the concept View research areas

What LDST captures

  • Hidden disease states that evolve over time
  • Trajectory shifts triggered by interventions
  • Early signals of worsening or improvement
  • Population-level patterns and subtypes

Overview

LDST connects data signals to meaningful clinical phases.

Latent states

Disease stages are inferred rather than observed directly, enabling earlier detection of progression and personalized monitoring strategies.

Trajectory maps

Visualize how individuals move between states, identifying common transition paths and high-risk turning points.

Dynamic modeling

Integrate temporal data (labs, imaging, wearable signals) with clinical events to model evolution over months or years.

How it works

A collaborative workflow for data, modeling, and clinical translation.

  1. Collect longitudinal data

    Aggregate EHR, biomarkers, imaging, and patient-reported outcomes.

  2. Infer hidden states

    Apply probabilistic models or deep learning to identify latent phases.

  3. Analyze transitions

    Estimate how interventions, demographics, or comorbidities shift trajectories.

  4. Deploy insights

    Share risk stratification dashboards and clinical decision support outputs.

Why it matters

LDST empowers both research and care delivery teams.

Earlier intervention

Spot subtle deterioration before symptoms are obvious.

Personalized treatment

Match patients to interventions based on their predicted trajectory.

Better clinical trials

Recruit participants based on trajectory stage to reduce trial noise.

Research focus areas

Key domains where latent trajectories are transforming care.

Neurodegenerative disease

Track subtle cognitive shifts and preclinical changes in imaging.

Cardiometabolic health

Identify early transitions to heart failure or diabetes complications.

Autoimmune conditions

Understand flare cycles and remission patterns at scale.
